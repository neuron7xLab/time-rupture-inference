# Manual Author Notes

A short, handwritten layer. Not generated filler, not an academic
costume. Read it as the reasoning behind the apparatus, not a pitch.

## 1. Why I built this artifact

I kept watching evaluation collapse into self-congratulation: a fluent
explanation of why a result is good, accepted because it reads well. I
wanted the opposite — a setup where a claim is admitted only because a
kill-test was written first, pinned by hash, run, and survived. The
deliverable here is that discipline, not a score. If the discipline is
sound, the specific numbers are almost incidental; if it is unsound, no
number would rescue it.

## 2. What I borrowed, and what I refuse to claim

Prediction-error language in this repository is an operational
inspiration, not a claim of biological fidelity. I borrowed one idea
from the predictive-coding lineage: a system can adapt by attending to
its own error rather than to a supervised target. That is the entire
debt. What runs is a scalar estimator with a sequential change trigger
— I refuse to dress it as anything resembling perception, mind, or
faithful biology. The relevant prior art is named and bounded in
`evidence/SOURCE_REGISTRY.yaml`; it is there to constrain my language,
not to borrow prestige.

## 3. Why negative results are preserved

I treat a failed falsifier as information, not as embarrassment. Every
RED — the poisoned cold-start, the precision-weighting that did not
pay, the learner that did not reach the floor — is sealed with a
reproduction command. A research line that hides its negatives is
indistinguishable from one that has none. The preserved lineage is the
most expensive and most honest thing in the repository, and it is the
part I would defend first under attack.

## 4. Why external reproduction is still open

I can run this on my machine; that is not reproduction. Until a person
who is not me clones the public repository, runs the gate without my
shell, and submits a sanitized proof bundle, `GAP_1` stays OPEN and the
readiness score is capped in code. I will not quietly relax that. An
unreproduced result is a hypothesis about my environment.

## 5. Why product readiness is not claimed

Two structural gaps — independent reproduction and domain breadth —
are open, and the code itself forbids a READY or productizable wording
while either is open. This is deliberate governance, not modesty
theatre. A synthetic benchmark that survives its own falsifiers is
evidence of method, not of market-ready software, and I would rather
under-state that than have a reviewer catch me over-stating it.

## 6. What would materially increase value next

In order: one author-absent external reproduction with a valid bundle;
then at least two structurally distinct task families with their own
pinned falsifiers and negative controls; then a surviving positive on
something that is not a synthetic interval family. Each of those moves
the artifact from "disciplined harness" toward "disciplined harness
with transferable evidence". Nothing else — more prose, more badges,
more citations — changes the real value.

## 7. What I refuse to claim

No intelligence, no understanding, no sentience, no biological
fidelity, no universal theory of time, no real-world validity, no
learned-model superiority, no hermetic or maximal supply-chain
security, and no novelty of falsification, change detection, or online
estimation. Those are old, named, and reused on purpose. The only
thing I claim as mine is the integration: fusing them into one
fail-closed apparatus where the cheap path — polishing a claim until
it sounds true — is blocked by a specific test rather than by my
goodwill.

If a reviewer finds a place where my language exceeds my mechanism,
that is a defect to report, and the lexicon gate exists precisely
because I do not trust my own future optimism either.
