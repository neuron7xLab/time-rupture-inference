# CLAIM BOUNDARY (enumerated, hard)

This document enumerates forbidden inferences; it is a negative
boundary, not positioning.

## Allowed claim grammar

> Under a pre-registered condition, the system passed/failed a specific
> executable test using a named metric over a stated window, producing
> a sealed artifact.

Nothing stronger is asserted anywhere outside an explicit disclaimer.

## Forbidden claims (never asserted)

- NOT cognition, consciousness, sentience, intelligence, or AGI.
- NOT a model of the brain, neurons, or biological neuroplasticity.
- NOT a universal theory of time or of perception.
- NOT a proof of any private theorem.
- NOT real-world validity (synthetic families only).
- NOT market value, productizable, or commercially validated.
- NOT generalization beyond the tested synthetic families.
- NOT "learned model superiority"; the v4 estimator is a single
  scalar.
- No "Dario / Ilya / Karpathy / frontier-lab level" framing — the
  artifact earns standing by gates, not by association.

## What IS claimed (scoped, artifact-backed)

- A frozen, byte-identical scalar prediction-error estimator beats a
  hard-wired and a best-naive baseline post-rupture on a synthetic
  benchmark (v4 invariant).
- A held-out-null-calibrated two-window contrast detects a synthetic
  rupture (incl. heavy-tail / bounded-carrier) without false-alarming
  on the plain null (lineages #26/#28, sealed).
- The adversarial battery rejects 8 degenerate probe classes.
- Every non-GREEN is preserved with a reproduction command.

The word "proven" appears only when bound to one of these specific
executable invariants. "causal" appears only for the v5 causal-action
lineage, which has a pinned falsifier and a bounded claim.
