# Limitations — What This Does Not Prove

Stated explicitly so the boundary is auditable rather than implied.
This page is the scope contract for the external review package.

## This does not prove

- **Cognition.** Nothing here is a model of thinking. The learner is
  a scalar estimate updated by its own error plus a drift trigger.
- **Consciousness or sentience.** Not addressed, not approached, not
  claimed under any reading.
- **Biological fidelity.** No neuron, synapse, or brain process is
  reproduced or approximated. Any "-like" marker is an operational
  measured quantity, never a biological claim.
- **General intelligence.** The scope is one hidden-regime
  temporal-rupture family and its constructed variants. Nothing
  generalizes beyond the synthetic families until tested.
- **A universal theory of time.** The object is a hidden inter-event
  interval with a regime rupture. It is a benchmark, not a physics or
  philosophy of time.
- **Startup-grade commercial value.** No market, adoption, or revenue
  claim is made. The artifact is a research instrument.
- **The correctness of any private theorem.** The engine tests whether
  a pinned claim survives its own falsifier. A survived check is
  evidence about *that check*, not a proof of the collaborator's
  theory. Asserting otherwise would itself violate the claim boundary.

## This does demonstrate

- A pre-registered claim can be hashed before the run, and a later
  threshold change is detectable through that hash.
- An adversarial battery mechanically rejects a non-deterministic
  probe, a non-finite metric, a decorative (non-load-bearing)
  threshold, and a non-discriminative negative control.
- A frozen positive is byte-identical and reproducible from a clean
  clone on any machine, with an ablation establishing necessity and a
  no-leakage check.
- Negative results are preserved as sealed artifacts with reproduction
  commands; no red was tuned to green; failed lineages are closed
  before new ones open.
- A private hypothesis can be run as a redacted skeleton plus a local
  opaque probe, with the mechanism, data, and theorem content never
  entering the public repository.
- A non-green outcome auto-proposes a decision-gated next experiment
  that tightens survivors and never loosens a failed check, and is
  never executed without a human gate.

## Open questions (framed as future experiments)

These are motivation and direction, not claims.

1. **Cross-domain probe transfer.** Does the redacted-skeleton +
   opaque-probe interface hold when the private mechanism is a
   non-temporal model (e.g. a graph or sequence estimator)? Experiment:
   register two structurally different private probes against the same
   skeleton and check that the engine remains mechanism-agnostic.

2. **Adversarial probe robustness.** Can a deliberately constructed
   probe pass the battery while encoding a hidden degenerate solution?
   Experiment: red-team the battery with probes designed to be
   pseudo-deterministic and pseudo-discriminative.

3. **Threshold-tightening convergence.** Under repeated non-green
   outcomes with ×0.9 tightening, does the proposed lineage converge to
   an informative boundary or collapse to an unattainable gate?
   Experiment: run the auto-proposal loop (human-gated) to fixpoint on
   a known-hard benchmark.

4. **Redacted-interface sufficiency.** Is there a class of research
   claim that cannot be expressed without leaking private structure
   into a never-share field? Experiment: attempt to encode several
   real private hypothesis shapes and log every field that resists
   redaction.

5. **Negative-control design cost.** How expensive is constructing a
   discriminative negative control for an arbitrary private probe, and
   does that cost dominate adoption? Experiment: measure
   skeleton-authoring time across distinct hypothesis types.

6. **Verdict-isolation under scale.** Does keeping research diagnostics
   out of the CI gate (so a legitimate red keeps CI green) remain
   sound as the number of concurrent private lineages grows?
   Experiment: stress the lineage ledger with many open negatives.

7. **Human-gate load.** At what experiment cadence does the mandatory
   human approval gate become the bottleneck, and is that a feature or
   a limit to be re-examined? Experiment: instrument approval latency
   over a multi-lineage run.
